15 Hidden Gems in South Tenerife That Tourists Don't Know About

📅 2026-04-20·⏱️ 15 min read

While most tourists flock to Los Cristianos, Playa de las Américas, and Costa Adeje, South Tenerife has countless hidden treasures waiting to be discovered. These are the places locals go — the secret beaches, authentic restaurants, and experiences that don't appear in guidebooks.

Staying at Ocean Deck House in Abades puts you in the heart of authentic South Tenerife, with many of these hidden gems just minutes away.

🏖️ Hidden Beaches

1. Playa de la Tejita

Distance from Abades: 15 km (15 min drive)
Best for: Long walks, kitesurfing, naturists

A long, golden sand beach near El Médano, popular with locals but often overlooked by tourists. The beach stretches for over a kilometre with the dramatic Montaña Roja (Red Mountain) at one end.

Why visit:

  • Natural, unspoiled, and rarely crowded
  • Excellent for kitesurfing and windsurfing
  • Clothing-optional section at the far end
  • Beach bars serving fresh fish
  • Stunning sunsets with views of Montaña Roja

Tip: Arrive early to avoid afternoon winds. The eastern end near Montaña Roja is more sheltered.

2. Playa de Abades

Distance from Ocean Deck House: 2 min walk
Best for: Snorkeling, families, authentic village atmosphere

Our local beach! A small, rocky cove with crystal-clear waters perfect for snorkeling. The marine reserve means abundant fish life just metres from shore.

Why visit:

  • Excellent snorkeling with visibility often exceeding 15 metres
  • Calm, protected waters ideal for children
  • Authentic fishing village atmosphere
  • Local seafood restaurants steps from the beach
  • Virtually no tourists

Tip: Bring snorkel gear (or borrow ours!) and explore the rocky areas on either side of the bay.

3. Playa de los Patos

Distance from Abades: 30 km (30 min drive)
Best for: Privacy, adventure, experienced swimmers

Hidden between rocks near Los Cristianos, this tiny beach is accessible only at low tide or by swimming around the rocks. Most tourists walk right past without knowing it's there.

Why visit:

  • Complete privacy — often deserted
  • Crystal-clear water perfect for snorkeling
  • Adventure to reach it
  • Dramatic rock formations

Warning: Check tide times before visiting. The beach disappears at high tide.

4. Playa de Antequera (Playa del Médano)

Distance from Abades: 18 km (18 min drive)
Best for: Windsurfing, kitesurfing, beach bars

The main beach at El Médano is technically not hidden, but it's surprisingly overlooked by package tourists who stick to the resort beaches. This is where locals and European expats come to surf, windsurf, and enjoy a more relaxed vibe.

Why visit:

  • Excellent wind conditions for water sports
  • Authentic beach town atmosphere
  • Great beach bars and restaurants
  • Long sandy beach perfect for walking
  • Vibrant international community

🏘️ Authentic Villages

5. Abades

Distance from Ocean Deck House: You're here!
Best for: Authentic Canarian life, seafood, snorkeling

Yes, we're biased, but Abades truly is a hidden gem! This tiny fishing village has maintained its authentic character while the rest of the south coast has been developed.

Don't miss:

  • The abandoned Sanatorio de Abona (10 min walk)
  • Fresh seafood at Restaurante El Caletón
  • Morning fish market at the small harbour
  • Snorkeling in the marine protected area
  • Sunset drinks on the waterfront

6. Vilaflor

Distance from Abades: 35 km (45 min drive)
Best for: Cool mountain air, hiking, local wine

The highest village in Spain at 1,400 metres altitude, surrounded by pine forests. A world away from the coastal heat.

Don't miss:

  • The Pino Gordo — an 800-year-old Canarian pine
  • Local wine tasting at Bodegas Chasna
  • Hiking trails through pine forests
  • Traditional Canarian architecture
  • Restaurante El Jable for local food

Tip: Bring a jacket — it can be 10°C cooler than the coast!

7. Arona Old Town

Distance from Abades: 25 km (30 min drive)
Best for: Traditional architecture, local markets, views

While Arona municipality includes the tourist areas of Los Cristianos and Playa de las Américas, the old town itself is authentically Canarian.

Don't miss:

  • Iglesia de San Antonio Abad — beautiful 18th-century church
  • Thursday morning market
  • Panoramic views over the south coast
  • Traditional Canarian houses with wooden balconies
  • Local bars serving cheap tapas

8. Arico Nuevo

Distance from Abades: 8 km (10 min drive)
Best for: Local food, wine, Guanche history

The main village of the Arico municipality, known for its wine production and traditional restaurants.

Don't miss:

  • Casa Tagoro — excellent Canarian food
  • Local wine from DO Abona vineyards
  • Guanche cave dwellings in the surrounding hills
  • Traditional plaza with old trees
  • Fiesta de San Juan in June (if you're here)

🍽️ Local Restaurants (Not Tourist Traps)

9. Tasca El Obispado (Granadilla de Abona)

Distance from Abades: 12 km (15 min drive)
Price: €€ (very affordable)
Best for: Traditional Canarian food, local atmosphere

A tiny local tasca serving traditional Canarian food at incredibly low prices. No English menu, no tourists — just locals and authentic food.

Must try:

  • Papas arrugadas con mojo (wrinkled potatoes with sauce)
  • Ropa vieja canaria (chickpea and meat stew)
  • Carne fiesta (marinated pork)
  • Local wine by the glass

Tip: Cash only. No reservations. Arrive early (1 PM for lunch, 8 PM for dinner).

10. Restaurante El Jable (El Médano)

Distance from Abades: 18 km (18 min drive)
Price: €€€
Best for: Fresh seafood, beachfront dining

Right on the beach at El Médano, this restaurant serves the freshest seafood with your toes in the sand.

Must try:

  • Pescado del día a la plancha (grilled fish of the day)
  • Pulpo a la gallega (Galician-style octopus)
  • Arroz negro (black rice with squid ink)
  • Vieja (parrotfish) — a Canarian specialty

Tip: Ask what came in that morning and order that.

11. Bar La Esquina (Abades)

Distance from Ocean Deck House: 3 min walk
Price: € (very cheap)
Best for: Tapas, local atmosphere, cold beer

Our local gem! A tiny bar with amazing tapas and the friendliest owner you'll ever meet.

Must try:

  • Chicharrones (fried pork belly)
  • Queso asado (grilled local cheese)
  • Croquetas (whatever the daily special is)
  • Carajillo (coffee with rum or brandy)

Tip: Don't expect a menu. Just ask what's good today.

12. Guachinche El Cordero (near Granadilla)

Distance from Abades: 15 km (20 min drive)
Price: € (very cheap)
Best for: Authentic experience, home-cooked food, local wine

A traditional guachinche — a family-run restaurant in someone's garage or backyard, serving home-cooked food and their own wine. This is as authentic as it gets.

What to expect:

  • No menu — they'll tell you what's cooking
  • Simple, hearty Canarian food
  • House wine served from barrels
  • Plastic tables and chairs
  • Locals only (until now!)

Tip: Only open certain months (traditionally November–March). Call ahead to check.

🏞️ Natural Wonders

13. Barranco del Infierno (Hell's Gorge)

Distance from Abades: 40 km (50 min drive)
Difficulty: Moderate
Duration: 3–4 hours round trip

A stunning hiking trail through a dramatic ravine ending at a waterfall (when there's water).

Why visit:

  • Spectacular volcanic landscapes
  • Lush vegetation in the gorge
  • Waterfall at the end (seasonal)
  • Well-maintained trail
  • Relatively uncrowded

Important:

  • Book in advance — limited daily visitors (300 max)
  • Book online at www.barrancodelinfiernonadeje.com
  • Bring water, sun protection, good shoes
  • Not suitable for young children

14. Montaña Amarilla (Yellow Mountain)

Distance from Abades: 22 km (25 min drive)
Difficulty: Easy
Duration: 1–2 hours

A volcanic cone near Costa del Silencio with lunar-like landscapes and panoramic views.

Why visit:

  • Otherworldly yellow and red volcanic rock
  • 360° views of the south coast
  • Easy, short hike suitable for all ages
  • Stunning sunset location
  • Free and uncrowded

Tip: Combine with a visit to nearby Playa de la Tejita.

15. Roque del Conde

Distance from Abades: 35 km (45 min drive)
Difficulty: Challenging
Duration: 4–5 hours round trip

A challenging hike to a dramatic rock formation with 360° views of South Tenerife.

Why visit:

  • Spectacular views from the summit (1,001m)
  • Dramatic volcanic landscapes
  • Sense of achievement
  • Relatively few hikers

Warning:

  • Steep, exposed trail — not for beginners
  • Bring plenty of water (2+ litres per person)
  • Start early to avoid midday heat
  • Good hiking boots essential

💡 Insider Tips

Getting Around

  • Rent a car — essential for reaching hidden spots
  • Use local buses (TITSA) — cheap and connect most villages
  • Avoid peak hours — traffic can be heavy near tourist areas (8–9 AM, 5–7 PM)
  • Download offline maps — mobile signal can be weak in rural areas

Best Times to Visit

  • Spring (March–May) — perfect weather, fewer tourists, lower prices
  • Autumn (September–November) — warm water, quiet beaches, still sunny
  • Avoid August — hottest month, most crowded, highest prices
  • Winter (December–February) — mild weather, escape cold Europe, whale watching season

Money-Saving Tips

  • Eat at local tascas instead of tourist restaurants (half the price, twice the quality)
  • Shop at local markets for fresh produce (Granadilla Saturday market, San Isidro Sunday market)
  • Stay in authentic villages like Abades instead of resort hotels
  • Visit free attractions like beaches, hiking trails, and viewpoints
  • Fill up at Disa petrol stations (usually cheapest)

Language

  • Learn basic Spanish — locals appreciate the effort
  • "Guachinche" = traditional family-run restaurant
  • "Papas arrugadas" = wrinkled potatoes (must-try!)
  • "Mojo" = traditional sauce (red or green)
  • "Gofio" = toasted grain flour (Canarian staple)

🌍 Respecting Local Culture

When visiting these hidden gems:

  • Support local businesses — buy from small shops and restaurants
  • Respect private property — not all beaches and trails are public
  • Take your trash — keep these places pristine
  • Be quiet — many are residential areas
  • Learn some Spanish — even basics are appreciated
  • Don't share exact locations on social media if they're truly hidden

Creating Your Itinerary

Day 1: Coastal Hidden Gems

  • Morning: Snorkel at Playa de Abades
  • Lunch: Bar La Esquina (Abades)
  • Afternoon: Visit Sanatorio de Abona
  • Sunset: Montaña Amarilla
  • Dinner: Restaurante El Jable (El Médano)

Day 2: Mountain Villages

  • Morning: Drive to Vilaflor
  • Lunch: Local restaurant in Vilaflor
  • Afternoon: Hike in pine forests or visit Pino Gordo
  • Evening: Return via Arona old town
  • Dinner: Tasca El Obispado (Granadilla)

Day 3: Adventure Day

  • Early morning: Barranco del Infierno hike (book ahead!)
  • Lunch: Picnic
  • Afternoon: Relax at Playa de la Tejita
  • Sunset: Beach bar at El Médano
  • Dinner: Fresh seafood at El Médano

Conclusion

South Tenerife offers so much more than the typical tourist experience. By venturing off the beaten path, you'll discover authentic Canarian culture, stunning natural beauty, and experiences that most visitors miss entirely.

Staying at Ocean Deck House in Abades puts you in the heart of authentic South Tenerife, with many of these hidden gems just minutes away. You'll experience the real Tenerife — the one locals know and love.
